The French Craft was delighted to propose, in partnership with the Mobilier national 's Laboratory for sustainable practices and the Big Bang Project agency, a day of exchanges around the issues of innovation and research in design and craft.

body: >-


On Thursday, December 12, 2024, many of you joined us for the launch of the Mobilier national's Laboratory for sustainable practices. Under the theme “National manufactures, sustainable manufactures”, this day of exchanges questioned the habitability of the world through 4 themes supported by expert speakers:
The first panel addressed materials and processes inspired by / based on nature, with Tony
Jouanneau ( Atelier Sumbiosis ), Aurélie Mossé ( Ensad Lab PSL Sacre ) and  Sandrine Rozier  (Textile designer textile), and was hosted by Carole Petitjean ( RDAI ).
The second exchange, “Tomorrow, start-ups in the arts and crafts?" gathered Katie
Cotellon ( Saint-Gobain Research Paris ), Bernard Leroy ( Agglomération Seine-Eure ), Thomas Paris ( HEC - CNRS ) and Éric Angiboust ( Muzéo ), and was hosted by Stefano
Micelli ( Université Ca' Foscari  - Homo Faber
Economy).
Third exchange, hosted by Marc Bayard ( Mobilier national ), addressed the redefinition of the contours of manual skills by digital technologies, with Julien Benayoun ( Bold Design ), Chloé Bensahel (Textile artist), Cyrile
Deranlot ( Daumet ), Laurent Gaveau (Culture
& Tech Consultant) et Anne Liberati (Consultant in color and trend forecasting).
The day ended with a debate on the necessary construction of collective imaginary worlds, bringing together  Marc Aurel (Designer), Ramy Fischler  (Designer), Aurélien
Fouillet (Sociologist) and Élodie Ternaux ( Hyloh ), also hosted by Marc Bayard .

On Thursday, December 12, 2024, many of you joined us for the launch of the Mobilier national's Laboratory for sustainable practices.

  • The first panel addressed materials and processes inspired by / based on nature, with Tony Jouanneau (Atelier Sumbiosis (external link)), Aurélie Mossé (Ensad Lab PSL Sacre (external link)) and Sandrine Rozier (external link) (Textile designer textile), and was hosted by Carole Petitjean (RDAI (external link)).
