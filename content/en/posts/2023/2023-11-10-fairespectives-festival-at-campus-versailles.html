An original creation of Campus Versailles, the Fairespectives festival spotlights craftsmen and heritage professionals committed to the ecological transition. For two days, debates, meetings, workshops and demonstrations invited professionals and the general public to see, understand and try out their projects and innovations.  

body: >-


This second edition of the festival aimed to demonstrate the crucial role of craft skills in the future of our societies, in the face of ecological challenges and the need for regional resilience. Bearers of the values of excellence, creativity, respect for the environment and diversity of local practices, these skills are major players in innovation, transmission and sustainability. The French Craft was naturally be present at this event on Saturday November 18th for stories, sharing and testimonials on these subjects:

“Bringing furniture back to the craftsman: a model for sustainable development?”  at 11:45am with Charles de Dainville and Henri Danzin de  Jaùh , publishers of contemporary handcrafted furniture.

"Designer's story: working with burnt wood"  at 2pm with  Sénéquier .

“Willing VS being able to conduct the ecological transition”  at 3pm with Pascale Lesage from  Arthylae .
