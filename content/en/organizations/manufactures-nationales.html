A unique public hub for the excellence of crafts and decorative arts.

body: >-


A public hub unique in the world, the Manufactures nationales have been working for the decorative arts for 400 years. Since 1740, the Manufacture de Sèvres has affirmed its vocation, both heritage and experimental in the ceramic arts. The Atelier de Recherche et de Création (ARC) of the Mobilier national, created in 1964 by André Malraux, produces furniture sets based on drawings by contemporary designers. The Laboratory of Sustainable Practices of the National Manufacturing Research and Innovation Directorate places art and design in a dynamic of research, innovation and eco-responsibility. It is supported by the Bettencourt Schueller Foundation.

AI-Crafts - Generative prototype
Since 1740, the Manufacture de Sèvres has affirmed its heritage and experimental vocation. A high-invoice creative laboratory, she is an important player in the art scene, design and decorative arts. It draws its strength from the excellence of its artisans and pasta, colours and enamels: unique know-how at the service of guest artists. AI-Crafts - Generative prototype is an interactive installation encourages visitors to mold a raw volume, which is promptly recorded by a camera. Generative artificial intelligence, trained on the Ruhlmann vase collection from the Manufacture nationale de Sèvres, subsequently offers formal interpretations that align with this heritage. The prototype consequently investigates innovative creative pathways, wherein AI depends on exceptional expertise to formulate credible hypotheses and rejuvenate forms.

In-Flexions, a duo formed by François Brument and Sonia Laugier, creates interactive devices and parametric objects. A pioneer of digital design in France, their works are featured at FNAC, Centre Pompidou, SFMoMA and MAD. In 2015, they founded Les Arts Codés and conducted their "Digital Crafts" research, hybridizing design, crafts and technology.

CHAISE LI "light innovation"
Composite chair of recycled carbon fibers pre impregnated with epoxy resin partly bio-sourced and with an expansive epoxy foam core. Seat covered with a blue leather in vegetable tanning taurillon (Tannerie Arnal) glued with aqueous base glue. Carbon recycling cycle by pyrolysis of industry production falls. The design of this chair is co-signed by Yanis Bontemps, Victor Galineau and Paul Seiller, three young designers graduated in 2021 from the École Nationale Supérieure des Arts Appliqués et des Métiers d'Art (ENSAAMA).
2.6 kg chair, stackable and compliant with NF EN 16139 - level 1 - chair for collective use Chair Technical Development: Manufactures nationales - ARC Chair Production: WorkShape
