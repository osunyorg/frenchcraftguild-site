The French Craft was delighted to propose, in partnership with the Mobilier national 's Laboratory of sustainable practices and Big Bang Project agency, a day of exchanges around the issues of innovation and research in design and craft.

body: >-


On Thursday, December 12, 2024, many of you joined us for the launch of the Mobilier national's Laboratory of sustainable practices. Under the theme “National Manufactures, Sustainable manufactures”, this day of exchanges questioned the habitability of the world through 4 themes supported by expert speakers:
The first round table addressed materials and processes inspired by / based on nature, with Tony Jouanneau ( Atelier Sumbiosis ), Aurélie Mossé ( Ensad Lab PSL Sacre ) and  Sandrine Rozier  (Textile designer), and was hosted by par Carole Petitjean ( RDAI ).
The second discussion, “Tomorrow, start-ups in the arts and crafts?” brought together Katie Cotellon ( Saint-Gobain Research Paris ), Bernard Leroy ( Agglomération Seine-Eure ), Thomas Paris ( HEC - CNRS ) and Éric Angiboust ( Muzéo ),  and was hosted by Stefano Micelli ( Université Ca' Foscari  - Homo Faber Economy).
The third discussion, moderated by Marc Bayard ( Mobilier national ), looked at how digital technologies are redefining the contours of manual skills, with Julien Benayoun ( Bold Design ),  Chloé Bensahel  (Textile artist), Cyrile Deranlot ( Daumet ), Laurent Gaveau (Culture & Tech Consultant ) et Anne Liberati ( Studio Liberati ).
The day ended with a debate on the necessary construction of collective imaginations, bringing together  Marc Aurel  (Designer),  Ramy Fischler  (Designer), Aurélien Fouillet (Sociologist) and Élodie Ternaux ( Hyloh ), also moderated by Marc Bayard.
